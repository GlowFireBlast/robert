2. Investigation

Research process

Researching the brief, I researched several areas related to forest monitoring and wildlife, which lead me to the idea of creating a camping environment assistant. This assistant would provide the user with real-time info about their logged surroundings, including weather condition updates, navigation assistance, and safety alerts.

Firstly, to investigate the needs of campers and the most crucual features for a camping environment assistant, I undertook several research tactics:

  • I researched the needs of campers and potential challenges they face while camping, deducing key factors such as weather conditions, navigation, and safety concerns.
  • I explored existing camping apps and tools, such as "Campy.app" to identify gaps in the market and potential features which could enhance user experience.
  • I conducted surveys and interviews with campers to gain insight into their preferences, which helped me understand the features they would find most useful in a camping environment assistant.
  • I researched the technologies available for developing such a tool with only school-level resources, such as the Microbit and Python, both of which I would ultimately use for both my embedded system and adaptive system components.

Key decisions informed by research

  • Theme choice: Camping environment assistant (environmental monitoring scenario), as it links directly to measurable environmental data (temperature, light, sound) and supports safety and awareness in wildlife forestry settings.
  • Inputs: Micro:bit sensors (temperature, light level, sound level) used as analog inputs to monitor environmental conditions. Buttons A and B used as digital inputs to trigger data logging and compass navigation functions.
  • Outputs: LED display and built-in speaker used to communicate info. LED shows compass directions, data logging confirmation and SOS messages, while the speaker provides an audible SOS emergency alert.
  • Data strategy: collect environmental sensor data using the Micro:bit, log to a CSV file, and process using Python to analyse conditions, consider user preference, and test "what-if" environmental scenarios.
