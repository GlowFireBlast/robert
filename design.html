3. Plan & Design

Design objectives

  • Build an embedded system using one or more digital input(s) and one or more analogue input(s), and generating one or more output(s).
  • Collect and store environmental data relevant to camping conditions (temperature, light level, sound level).
  • Monitor environmental conditions and detect potential disaster risks (e.g. high temperature leading to forest fire risk).
  • Build a Python program to process logged data and determine optimal camping conditions.
  • Include "what-if" scenario testing to compare how environmental changes (e.g. temperature increase due to drought conditions) affect camping suitability.
  • Add an adaptive feedback mechanism where the system displays warnings or alerts when unsafe conditions are detected.

Stakeholders and end-user needs

  • Campers/Hikers: need a reliable system to monitor environmental conditions and receive alerts about potential risks to ensure their safety and enhance their camping experience.
  • Outdoor beginners/environmentalists: benefit from guidance on whether based on their preference, the current conditions are suitable for their camping experience.
  • Students/researchers: want information logged environmental data to analyse and compare different scenarios.

Technologies Used

  • Embedded: Microbit with built-in sensors (temperature, light, sound) and external components (LEDs, buttons).
  • Software: Python for data processing, analysis, and user interface development.
